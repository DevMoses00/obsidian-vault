##  What's An Essay For in The Age of AI - George Dillard


*"Running, I guess, is a thing of the past. I should stop my runs and just zip around my neighborhood in my car a few mornings a week. I’ll be so much better off, right?*

*If I did so, I’d be following the logic of [all](https://www.timeshighereducation.com/depth/have-chatbots-killed-student-essay) [the](https://www.theatlantic.com/technology/archive/2022/12/chatgpt-ai-writing-college-student-essays/672371/) [people](https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2025/07/07/the-end-of-the-english-paper) who are declaring the “end of the essay.” AI can spit out 1000 words on the French Revolution more efficiently than a…"*

- We think the frictionless of emerging technology will just solve our problems and make us better, while we forget that the act of writing is a skill that we practice not just to write better essays for class, but to be better thinkers, readers, and writers - to understand the world around us and be able to put it into words.


The rest of the article is paywalled, but the general idea is that it is right to do hard things because you can get better at them, and that the skills and benefits tied to that hard thing will yield much more reward than the comfort and ease of ai.
